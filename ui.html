Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun, with a very thin atmosphere and extreme temperature variations. Its surface is covered with craters, resembling our Moon.

Venus

Venus is similar in size to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere mainly composed of carbon dioxide. It has extreme greenhouse effects, making it the hottest planet in the solar system.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life, with diverse ecosystems, abundant water, and a protective atmosphere. It has one natural satellite, the Moon.

Mars

Mars, also known as the Red Planet, has a thin atmosphere and is home to the tallest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons. Scientists are exploring the possibility of past life on Mars.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system. It has a strong magnetic field and dozens of moons, including the largest, Ganymede. Its Great Red Spot is a massive storm larger than Earth.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock particles. It has many moons, including Titan, which has lakes of liquid methane and ethane.

Uranus

Uranus has a unique tilt, rotating on its side compared to other planets. It has a blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere and faint rings surrounding it.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and is known for its deep blue color and supersonic strong winds. It has faint rings and 14 known moons, the largest being Triton.

Pluto

Pluto, now classified as a dwarf planet, has a heart-shaped glacier and is located in the Kuiper Belt. It has five known moons, with Charon being the largest.
